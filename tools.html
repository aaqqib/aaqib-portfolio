A curated and growing list of SEO, design and development resources I use regularly and find useful.

Recently Added

SEO

Ahrefs
The ultimate all-in-one SEO toolset. I use it primarily for deep backlink analysis, competitor research, and tracking keyword visibility across major clients.
Semrush
My go-to for comprehensive site audits, domain authority checks, and discovering highly profitable 'striking distance' keywords.
Screaming Frog SEO Spider
The industry-standard crawler. Indispensable for identifying broken links, analyzing page titles & meta data, and discovering deep technical SEO issues.
Google Analytics 4
The absolute source of truth for measuring traffic and events. Critical for setting up custom conversions and understanding user journeys.
Google Search Console
Direct insight into how Google views your site. Essential for checking index coverage and actual query performance.
Figma
The collaborative interface design tool. I use it for wireframing new page templates before they go into development to ensure SEO constraints are met.
VS Code
My preferred lightweight code editor. Perfect for making quick HTML/CSS adjustments to client sites or editing structured data schemas.
GitHub
Version control platform. Essential for tracking code changes when collaborating with developer teams on technical SEO implementations.
Notion
The all-in-one workspace. I manage my entire content calendar, keyword research databases, and client deliverables here.
Slack
The standard for async communication. Vital for staying connected with remote clients and digital marketing teams.
